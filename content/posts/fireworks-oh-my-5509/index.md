I have stooped to a new low.&nbsp; You may be asking, &quot;What about Ana?&quot;&nbsp; She would be disappointed in me, that's what.&nbsp; Why?&nbsp; Reasons. Complicated reasons.&nbsp; Californication is playing in the background, and I am very content.&nbsp; Why? I am sitting in a KFC in The Hague, typing on my PDA while somebody speaks to me in another language about it.&nbsp; How disappointing.

We arrived on the 14th at about 11:30 p.m.&nbsp; The train ride took a while, with a few transfers and one delay attributed to &quot;air conditioning.&quot;&nbsp; I could live with it. Well...

Phew!&nbsp; It is now the 18th, and a lot has happened since that &quot;well.&quot;&nbsp; Let me backtrack to where I was.

We had to switch trains. Another great story!

Back to the story.&nbsp; We arrived rather late, and all the hostels and hotels were full.&nbsp; Another traveller joined us in looking for a hostel, but we had no luck.&nbsp; A man approached and asked whether we needed a place to stay. We did, although the arrangement seemed questionable.&nbsp; When we encountered him again, we eventually agreed.&nbsp; We stayed at his apartment for about 30 euros each.&nbsp; It was expensive, but we had to sleep somewhere.&nbsp; The next morning we left, checked our bags at the airport, and wandered around the city.&nbsp; We walked through the red-light district, but Amsterdam was not really my city. People were smoking weed everywhere, and it simply wasn't my scene.&nbsp; At about 6:30 that evening, I boarded a train to The Hague, where I had reservations and everything.&nbsp; I checked into my expensive hostel and left immediately.

Which brings me to KFC.&nbsp; As I was finishing the earlier &quot;well,&quot; two men started speaking English with me, and we chatted for a while.&nbsp; They were very kind and offered to take me on an unofficial official tour.&nbsp; We walked through the main part of town, saw the government buildings, and visited the embassy district.&nbsp; It was quite a good tour.&nbsp; We ended up at one of their flats and had a few beers.&nbsp; Luckily, one of them was a computer enthusiast like me, so we talked about technology for a while, which always brings people together.&nbsp; They asked whether I was going to the fireworks they had mentioned earlier.

I said I would try to make it, and they invited me to join them.&nbsp; We boarded a crowded tram to the pier, made our way through thousands of people, and sat on the beach.&nbsp; Sweden's display began at 10:00 and the Netherlands' at 11:00 as part of a European fireworks competition.&nbsp; The displays were amazing. Between them, we visited a casino, where they both won a little money.&nbsp; After the second display, we wandered to a bar, ordered drinks, and finished them quickly.&nbsp; I ended up running back to the hostel because of its 1:00 a.m. curfew.

The next day I woke fairly early, found a grocery store, ate, returned to the hostel, and slept for the entire afternoon.&nbsp; I woke at 8:00 p.m., got ready, and met Rene and Sytze at 9:00.&nbsp; We had a few more beers and returned to the beach for another fireworks display. Afterward, we drank a considerable amount of vodka.&nbsp; Eventually we returned to their apartment, met some of their friends, and had a few more beers. They smoked some hash, we laughed, and Rene fell asleep. Later, Sytze gave me a lift back on his bicycle.&nbsp; The next morning I woke early to the sounds of another guest, packed my things, and checked out.&nbsp; I took a train to Rotterdam, where I arrived an hour and a half later. I walked around, took a few pictures, then returned to Amsterdam and boarded an overnight train to Copenhagen.&nbsp; I shared a compartment with two people from Portugal and a couple from Sweden.&nbsp; Now I am in Copenhagen, about to go cycling. Goodbye!

Unfortunately, not much has happened in the last couple of days.&nbsp; So, let's see: what on earth is going on?&nbsp; My last entry was from the train from Zurich to Milan.&nbsp; In Milan I jumped on a train to Bologna, only to discover that I needed a reservation.&nbsp; Oops!&nbsp; The conductor was frustrated and scolded me in Italian.&nbsp; She eventually said, &quot;I'll let you pass this time,&quot; or something like that, since I only had 9 euros and the ticket cost 16.&nbsp; Never again will I jump on a Eurostar train without checking; it was not worth the hassle.&nbsp; I ended up in Bologna, checked my email, wrote long messages to everybody, and eventually caught a rough night train south. The train was not comfortable, but it went to B. Batista.&nbsp; That was the last night of my roughly four-day stretch without proper sleep.&nbsp; The world was starting to feel dizzy, which was unfortunate.&nbsp; I ended up in Brindisi and met a few more people: Mark, Niko, and Marny, all very Australian!&nbsp; We all boarded a ferry to a little island (Corfu, Mark adds) and are now staying at the Pink Palace.&nbsp; It is rather chaotic, but not my kind of chaos. I am thinking about staying for only one night, and perhaps another on the return trip.&nbsp; I am enjoying everybody here; they are quite delightful.&nbsp; I considered staying another night, but would really like to see Ana. I don't quite know why I miss her, but I do.&nbsp; We all had a few drinks and played some pool.&nbsp; I still have plenty of room to improve.&nbsp; Now I am back and getting ready to sleep.&nbsp; Good night; more interesting stories when we talk again.

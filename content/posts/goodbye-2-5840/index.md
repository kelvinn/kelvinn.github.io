[I wrote the following in the U.S. to sum up my trip.]

I finally arrived in Munich and quickly caught a train to Venice.&nbsp; I met Christy in Venice early in the morning, and we wandered around all day.&nbsp; She showed me her hostel, which was nice but expensive, and then the rest of the city.&nbsp; I was surprised by how dirty Venice seemed.&nbsp; I had imagined a romantic city, and although the canals were amazing, it did not feel especially clean.&nbsp; Not Amsterdam dirty, but not exactly pristine.&nbsp; I caught a train with Christy to Milan, where she ended up missing her plane, but that is another story.&nbsp; While in Venice, I had discovered that there were no hostel beds available anywhere from Rome to Germany.&nbsp; I needed to be in Zurich by 9:00 the next night, so I couldn't stray too far.&nbsp; I decided to return to Interlaken and sleep beside its beautiful lake.&nbsp; Unfortunately, I took a wrong train and ended up in a town whose name I didn't even know.&nbsp; It was late, so I wandered into the hills, found a large field, and simply went to sleep.&nbsp; It was one of the best nights of sleep I had on the trip. The field had soft grass and was wonderfully peaceful.&nbsp; I woke the next morning and, although it was raining, wandered back to the train and made my way to Zurich.&nbsp; I spent the day walking around and buying a few last things before eventually boarding my plane.

I arrived in London around 10:00 p.m. and really wanted to see Ana one last time; she was staying at a hostel downtown.&nbsp; After being asked to leave the airport, I made my way into the city to find her.&nbsp; I went to Compton Place, even though the hostel was on Compton Street, and walked around until 1:00 in the morning.&nbsp; The hostel was nowhere to be seen, and my sleeping options were looking limited.&nbsp; I examined a few bushes as possibilities.&nbsp; I then spoke with a young woman who offered some advice. She eventually said, &ldquo;If you need a place to stay, here is my number.&rdquo;&nbsp; At about 1:30 I called, and she welcomed me into her home.&nbsp; I curled up on her couch and, once again, slept remarkably well.&nbsp; The next morning she took me out to breakfast before I went to the airport.

I arrived fairly early and began checking in.&nbsp; When I was asked, &ldquo;Where did you stay last night?&rdquo; the complications began.&nbsp; I was selected for additional screening, thorough enough that it felt as though it would have stopped a pin from getting onto the aircraft.&nbsp; The especially humorous moment came when staff saw a few of the books I had packed, whose titles I will not specify, and one of them gave me a few amused looks.&nbsp; Everything was scanned, my bag was searched, and of course nothing was found.&nbsp; None of this would have mattered except that Ana was also waiting for a flight, which we had arranged to depart just an hour before mine.&nbsp; I ran to her gate, somehow moved through security quickly, and found that they were already boarding.&nbsp; She was about to get on but said, &ldquo;I knew you would come!&rdquo; We waited until the final boarding call and had one of the most romantic goodbyes ever: kisses, hugs, tears, and declarations of love.&nbsp; It was lovely.&nbsp; She boarded her flight to Madrid, and I ran back to my gate for my flight to St. Louis and then Portland.&nbsp; After a long journey, I arrived home, where Casey picked me up at the airport.&nbsp; I finished another book on the plane, but I was home.

So, that was my trip to Europe.&nbsp; Adventurous? Of course.&nbsp; Entertaining? You bet.&nbsp; Life-changing? Certainly.&nbsp; I'll return and spend more time there.&nbsp; I am still in contact with several people from the trip, including Ana and my friends from Sweden.

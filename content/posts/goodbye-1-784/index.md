I am alone again, on an ICE to Venice.

After going to the top of the Eiffel Tower, we returned to the hostel, made some food, and went to bed.&nbsp; I fell asleep fairly early, while Ana stayed up longer to write in her journal.&nbsp; The next morning, we did not really know what to do. I needed to reach Venice eventually, while she needed to visit Amsterdam and London.&nbsp; Once awake, we wandered around Paris for quite a while, got some food, and had some film developed.&nbsp; She called her parents a few times, and we went to the train station to make reservations. We had also visited the Moulin Rouge the previous night, by the way.&nbsp; Eventually, I emailed my friend Christy to say that I would not reach Venice the next day but would arrive on the morning of the 10th.&nbsp; Ana and I collected our belongings and went to the train station at 23:00 for the overnight train to Amsterdam.&nbsp; We arrived early the next morning and wandered around for a few hours.&nbsp; We returned to the Red Light District, had breakfast, then spent several hours at the Van Gogh Museum.

Ana loves Van Gogh, so you can imagine how happy she was.&nbsp; We went back to the station because my train left at 15:00 and hers at 16:00.&nbsp; I boarded my train, we said goodbye, and I left.&nbsp; I hoped to see her again in London because our flights departed from the same area, although I did not know whether our paths would cross.&nbsp; My journey took me from Amsterdam to Frankfurt, then Munich, followed by an overnight train to Venice.&nbsp; One interesting thing did happen on the way south: a group of Scotsmen effectively took over several train carriages, including mine.&nbsp; They were starting their celebrations early for a football match.

Occasionally, bagpipes sounded over the train's intercom, and at one point the entire carriage sang a Scottish song.&nbsp; It was an amusing experience, although I was exhausted after the previous night and all the walking in Paris.&nbsp; Each day there, we had needed to change rooms by 09:15 because the hostel was nearly full.&nbsp; Oh well.&nbsp; I am now almost in Munich, and soon I will be home.&nbsp; Time to email Casey and make sure he can pick me up; he owes me at least that much.&nbsp; I also need to find a beer mug for him...

Ah, an idea!

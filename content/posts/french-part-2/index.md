I've had a long history of poorly learning languages. In school I learned Spanish, and following the normal school curriculum did average - at best. A few years later I tried to learn Mandarin, and while easier in some ways, it was significantly harder in other ways. I could get by and order food and follow directions, but not much more than that. A number of years later I tried to learn French, and in some ways was more successful, but I still don't think I ever got above about the A2/B1 level.

At least with French I generally enjoyed studying. Fluent Forever was a great read. That was 10 years ago now.

One of my challenges has been being oversubscribed in other areas, but perhaps I'm just making excuses. You can see that I haven't written up a new "project" in over 10 years - life, right?

Let's try again. Why now? Because I think I can tailor a learning system around my constraints.

1) I work full-time, and sometimes up to 60hr weeks for 5-6 weeks in a stretch.
2) I workout or run every morning
3) I have a kid
4) I work at my computer, so would prefer to limit the time I spend at my desk

This post is partially to write down the approach I'm planning on starting with. It is inspired by Fluent Forever, and combined perhaps with MT/Pimsleur.

With each lesson:

- I'll use ChatGPT to generate each lesson or graded reader, which I will aim to take about one hour. It will specifically use the vocabulary I know, and keep me in the *goldilocks zone*.
- During some sessions I will use ChatGPT Voice to practice speaking
- This lesson will then be fed into ElevenLabs to generate audio, and I'll have each audio file as a podcast. 
- That same text will be fed into Anki pulling out the most important vocabulary or sentences.
- Periodically I'll generate a graded reader and send to my kindle, and read that on the bus or before bed instead of just audio.

I'll aim to fine tune the above system, and update here how it has changed. My goal then is 1hr per day, mostly during my run/workout, but also with some flash card studying on the side. 

Wish me luck.

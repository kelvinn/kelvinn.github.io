I've had a long history of struggling to learn languages. I studied Spanish at school and, following the standard curriculum, achieved average results at best. A few years later I tried to learn Mandarin. It was easier in some ways and significantly harder in others. I could get by, order food, and follow directions, but not much more. Several years later I tried French and was more successful in some respects, although I still don't think I progressed beyond roughly the A2/B1 level.

At least with French I generally enjoyed studying. Fluent Forever was a great read. That was 10 years ago now.

One of my challenges has been being overcommitted in other areas, although perhaps I'm just making excuses. I haven't written up a new "project" in more than 10 years. Life, right?

Let's try again. Why now? Because I think I can tailor a learning system around my constraints.

1. I work full-time, sometimes up to 60 hours a week for five or six weeks at a stretch.
2. I work out or run every morning.
3. I have a child.
4. I work at a computer, so I prefer to limit the additional time I spend at my desk.

This post is partly an attempt to document the approach I plan to start with. It is inspired by Fluent Forever, perhaps combined with MT and Pimsleur.

With each lesson:

- I'll use ChatGPT to generate each lesson or graded reader, designed to take about one hour. It will specifically use the vocabulary I know and keep me in the *Goldilocks zone*.
- During some sessions, I will use ChatGPT Voice to practise speaking.
- I'll then feed the lesson into ElevenLabs to generate audio and make each audio file available as a podcast.
- I'll feed the same text into Anki, extracting the most important vocabulary or sentences.
- Periodically, I'll generate a graded reader, send it to my Kindle, and read it on the bus or before bed instead of relying only on audio.

I'll aim to fine-tune the system and document how it changes. My goal is one hour per day, mostly during my run or workout, with some flashcard study on the side.

Wish me luck.

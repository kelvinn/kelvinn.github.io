When the contractions started, we put our delivery plan into action. We grabbed our bags, headed out the front door, and walked about one kilometre to the hospital. This was the start of a relatively unusual life for our daughter.

At about three months old, we sold our apartment and moved to New Zealand, where I could do some consulting. A serviced apartment? Yes, please.

When our daughter was about 10 months old, we swapped roles, packed a single bag for the three of us, flew to Europe, travelled for a while, and then found an apartment in France. I spent the next year as a stay-at-home dad, with three goals and one optional stretch goal:

* Learn how to cook a little better.
* Learn how to sharpen knives.
* Spend time with my daughter.
* Optional: learn a little French.

With almost no other concerns in life, I was able to spend a lot of time with my daughter, and I was fortunate enough to find some great books on child development and learning. After a year in France, we packed our bags, travelled a bit more, visited family in the United States, and then arrived back in Australia. My daughter had visited 22 or 23 countries before her second birthday.

So, what do I know about parenting? I do not claim expertise on parenting forums, and I must admit that I do not even know what DS, DD, DA, or DH means there. Instead, I will offer this: at five years old, my daughter is calm and easygoing, and I genuinely enjoy having her around. It has not been perfect, but here is what worked well and what did not.

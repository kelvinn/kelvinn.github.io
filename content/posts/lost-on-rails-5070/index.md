Well, I woke up and got a little food from a pastry shop. It was so good.&nbsp; I checked my bag at the train station, while Sibyl and Ash kept theirs at the hotel.&nbsp; At about noon, we caught a train to Monaco, then a bus to Genoa (I think), followed by another train. This is where things started to get interesting.&nbsp; In Genoa, we boarded a train headed toward one of the Cinque Terre towns, but I climbed into a different section from Sibyl and Ashley.&nbsp; The train travelled one stop and then simply stopped.&nbsp; I met two women from Florida, Rosie and Marxy, and we claimed some first-class seats.&nbsp; After a while, the train began travelling in the opposite direction.&nbsp; Oh no!&nbsp; A few other passengers were also wondering what was happening.

We spoke to the conductor and learned that the train was heading toward a town in France, not where we wanted to go.&nbsp; I got off in Genoa again and, ten minutes later, boarded another train to La Spezia, which would drop us at one of the Cinque Terre towns.&nbsp; I&#39;m now sitting with the two women from Florida, having travelled for six hours across southern France and into Italy.&nbsp; Looking back, I really enjoyed the bus ride through the small towns.&nbsp; The scenery along the way was awe-inspiring, with the French Riviera looking just as it does in the books.&nbsp; The ocean is amazing.&nbsp; Now I&#39;m on the last train to my destination, which somehow seems to be spending more time in tunnels than above ground.&nbsp; So I bid farewell, not knowing where my travelling companions are and facing a 90% chance that I will be sleeping on the beach tonight and showering in the ocean tomorrow.

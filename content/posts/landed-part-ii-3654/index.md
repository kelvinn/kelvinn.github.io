Remembering quotes from Camus, I realize a couple of things.&nbsp; First, I am about one hour outside London, and second, I really don&#39;t have any idea what date it is.&nbsp; I think it is the 15th, but we are about 24 hours into our 34-hour journey to Barcelona.&nbsp; The flight has been relatively uneventful: Ashley slept for much of it, while I read more than 200 pages from various books.&nbsp; My sleep has been anything but regular; I have had no more than three hours a night for the past five days or so.&nbsp; I had to leave Portland with a &quot;bang&quot;!

Jumping back slightly, Ashley and I left her apartment at 4:00am and got on the MAX.&nbsp; At 3:45 I called Sarah, a friend from New Zealand.&nbsp; It was good to hear her accent again.&nbsp; Soon we were at the airport, early enough to make it through the very long security lines.&nbsp; Our first flight went well too.&nbsp; St. Louis was amusing, with a four-hour delay that became six.&nbsp; Low turbulence, straightforward flying: a smooth trip so far.&nbsp; Let&#39;s hope the rest of the journey goes as planned.&nbsp; In St. Louis we met a woman named Dawn, who was travelling from Florida to visit her 18-year-old daughter in Rome.&nbsp; We talked for a while about travel and life.&nbsp; She happened to be on the same flight to London and is sitting in row 38 (we are in 35).&nbsp; She became a mother at 17 and seems to have raised an amazing daughter with whom she has a close relationship.&nbsp; The perseverance of so many people is remarkable.&nbsp; But alas, the food just arrived, and there is no keeping Kelvin from that!

I think the date in my last entry was slightly wrong, since I spent only three days in Copenhagen. I think it was really the 17th, but I don&#39;t know for certain!

I have now been in Europe for well over a month, and my time is winding down.&nbsp; I have most of the remainder planned out, which is nice, but first, a summary of what happened in Copenhagen:

In my last entry, I mentioned that I went biking, which I did for almost the entire day. Pat, a guy from San Francisco, and I biked around town for roughly six hours. There were not too many highlights, apart from a great series of lakes in Copenhagen with a path running around them. We rode rented, rather battered bikes that cost 20 kroner each, or about three euros. You insert a coin, release a bike, and off you go. It took us an hour and a half to find two of them, but once we did, we biked all over the city.

We went to Christiania, also known as Freetown Christiania. It felt like a smaller, less regulated Amsterdam. Cannabis was openly available, and the police generally stayed out; an attempt to enter had reportedly sparked a riot. It was an interesting place. We then rode back and borrowed hair clippers from a woman at a restaurant called Govinda&#39;s whom I had mentioned in my last entry.

That night, we spent about two hours shaving my head because the clippers were not very good, then shaved Pat&#39;s head completely smooth. Quite an adventure. I met Hilary from Portland and Sarah from Minnesota, both of whom enter the haircut story. My head was shaved with a No. 2 guard, except the back was not fully trimmed. I was the only one who had shaved a head before, so nobody else really knew what to do.

A French guy tidied the back a little, but it still needed more work. I told Sarah about the time Molly had trimmed the back of my hair almost up to my ears, and Sarah said she could not possibly do that. I handed her the clippers and, zzzzz, she cut a line all the way to the top of my head. I could hardly believe it.

I had joked that Molly&#39;s earlier effort had ruined my dating prospects for a month, so when I felt this new line, I said, &quot;There go my dating prospects for another month.&quot; Hilary laughed from inside the shower, while Pat came out, saw it, and started laughing too. We simply buzzed my head with a No. 1 guard, which is very close. Good times. Lizz would love my hair.

I wrote a few emails that night, returned to the hostel, played cards with the three of them for a while, and went to bed. I woke at noon the next day and, after eating and taking a shower, went to the internet cafe and played games for a while. I looked around town a little more, then went to bed.

At the internet cafe, I managed to contact Maria and Kerstin, friends I had met on Ios. When they heard that I was coming to Sweden, they insisted that I visit, so I made plans to meet them.

This morning, I checked out of the hostel and returned to the internet cafe, where I played more games and met another guest who also played BF1942. We played a few rounds together; I won early, but he caught up eventually. I then returned to the hostel to collect my belongings and catch the train. I had lost track of the time, but I jumped on a bus and reached the station just in time. I am now on the X2000, a high-speed train to Stockholm, followed by another train to Falun, where they live. Hurrah!

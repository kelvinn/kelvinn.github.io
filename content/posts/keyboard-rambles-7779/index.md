I am quite glad to be on an American keyboard; European keyboards take some getting used to!&nbsp; So, let&#39;s see: life is indeed quite interesting.&nbsp; After taking a long nap, I woke up to two women moving in. Being my outgoing self, I asked, &quot;So, where are you both from?&quot; That is where it all started.&nbsp; They joined a few friends and me on our adventures for the night.&nbsp; They were both charming, but both were already seeing people.&nbsp; But on to the story.

We left at about 8:30pm and met up with friends at, guess where, Cafe Zurich!&nbsp; We waited around for another friend and went to the grocery store to get some food and drinks.&nbsp; Good times were had by all.&nbsp; We bought a few bottles of wine and some schnapps, jumped on the metro, got off one stop too early, and missed a bus.&nbsp; That was when the real adventure began.&nbsp; We followed some other people to the wrong place while trying to see  Amelie at a large outdoor theatre.&nbsp; We took a taxi up the hill, found the screening sold out, walked back down after having a drink at the top, discovered the metro was closed, took a taxi most of the way home, and walked the rest of the way.&nbsp; It is now about two in the morning, and I am ready to sleep.&nbsp; I am debating whether to shower tonight or in the morning. We will see when my roommate comes back.

I wonder how loud these keys are as I type, but it probably does not matter.&nbsp; Another slightly frustrating discovery: one of the women is a total techie. Why do I meet people I connect with so well when the timing is never quite right?&nbsp; Who knows; I am sure I will meet the right person eventually.&nbsp; They laughed when I wanted some bread before bed, but I am quite hungry right now.&nbsp; My quote book is going well and has helped me make friends with several people.&nbsp; The people I keep meeting have made the difficult parts of travel much easier.&nbsp; I cannot wait until I am travelling on my own and meeting people from all over the world, though it is probably best done in stages.&nbsp; I have not experienced much culture shock because I am with friends who help me find my way, but I am sure travelling alone will be quite different.&nbsp; I think we will go out again tomorrow night.&nbsp; I also made friends with someone whose name I do not know after giving her a large bottle of water. She was asking for change to buy some downstairs, and I simply said, &quot;Here, have this.&quot; She mentioned being dehydrated, and I certainly know that feeling.&nbsp; I told her to buy water at the supermarkets from now on, since it costs about 0.19 euros rather than one euro, and the bottle is twice as large.&nbsp; Quite a saving!&nbsp; I think I am going to brush my teeth and curl up in bed; that sounds very good right now.&nbsp; Good night for now, my friend, until our next encounter!
